Hedgehog

Hedgehogs earned their name from their preferred habitat - garden hedges - and the pig-like grunts they make when noisily snuffing around undergrowth in search of food.

  • Linguistics: A group of hedgehogs is called an "array." A baby hedgehog is a "hoglet."
  • Lifespan: 4-7 years
  • Speed: Up to 12mph
  • Defense: When threatened, hedgehogs roll into a tight ball to protect their head and underbelly.

Hedgehogs are known as the "gardener's friend" as they can often be found foraging hedges and gardens for beetles and caterpillars. There are 17 species of hedgehogs, but none are native to America or Australia.
